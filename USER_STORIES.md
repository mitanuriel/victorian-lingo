# Victorian Lingo — User Stories

> An immersive Victorian and Regency era English learning app powered by Project Gutenberg.
> Stories are organized by epic. Each story follows the format:
> **As a [persona], I want to [action], so that [benefit].**

---

## Personas

| Persona | Description |
|---|---|
| **The Reader** | Wants an immersive reading experience in era-appropriate style |
| **The Word Enthusiast** | Collects and learns Victorian/Regency vocabulary |
| **The Scholar** | Wants depth — etymology, historical context, anachronism detection |
| **The Quizzer** | Motivated by gamified drills, flashcards, and measurable progress |

---

## Epic 1 — Library & Book Discovery

**US-001** — As a Reader, I want to browse a curated catalog of Victorian and Regency era
classics, so that I can discover works appropriate to the period without having to search
the wider internet.

**US-002** — As a Reader, I want to search for books by title, author, or subject keyword,
so that I can quickly find a specific work I have in mind (e.g., "Dickens", "sensation
novel", "manners").

**US-003** — As a Reader, I want the app to fetch books automatically from Project
Gutenberg via the Gutendex API, so that I always have access to legal, free, full texts
without manual downloads.

**US-004** — As a Reader, I want books to be cached locally after first download, so that
I can read without an internet connection.

**US-005** — As a Reader, I want to filter the catalog by era (Regency 1811–1820,
Victorian 1837–1901), genre (gothic, sensation, society novel, poetry), or author, so
that I can narrow my choices meaningfully.

**US-006** — As a Reader, I want to see basic metadata for each book — author, year of
publication, a brief synopsis, and a reading difficulty indicator — so that I can make an
informed choice before starting.

---

## Epic 2 — Reader Experience

**US-007** — As a Reader, I want to read texts rendered in a paper-like, era-appropriate
visual format (aged parchment texture, Victorian serif typography, decorative chapter
headers), so that the reading experience feels immersive and thematically consistent.

**US-008** — As a Reader, I want to tap or long-press any word while reading to reveal a
contextual panel with its definition, era-specific usage notes, and part of speech, so
that I never need to leave the app to look up an unfamiliar term.

**US-009** — As a Reader, I want archaic, era-specific, and unusual words automatically
highlighted (with a subtle ink-wash underline) as I read, so that my attention is drawn
to vocabulary worth learning.

**US-010** — As a Reader, I want my reading progress tracked per book (percentage read,
last position), so that I can resume exactly where I left off across sessions.

**US-011** — As a Reader, I want to adjust text size within Victorian-style font options,
so that I can tailor legibility to my preference without breaking the visual theme.

**US-012** — As a Reader, I want a sepia, candlelight, and dark ("midnight ink") reading
mode, so that I can read comfortably in different lighting conditions.

---

## Epic 3 — Vocabulary & Word Learning

**US-013** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want era-specific definitions sourced or annotated
for Victorian/Regency usage (not just modern dictionary entries), so that I understand
words as the original readers would have.

**US-014** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want the app to flag interesting vocabulary in
three categories — **Archaic** (obsolete today), **Period Slang** (Victorian cant,
drawing-room vocabulary), and **False Friend** (words whose meaning has shifted
significantly) — so that I understand the nuance of each.

**US-015** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want a daily "Word of the Era" — a curated
period-specific term with definition, example sentence from the corpus, and
etymology — so that I learn new vocabulary every day even outside active reading.

**US-016** — As a Scholar, I want to see a word's etymology chain (e.g., Latin root →
Old French → Middle English → Victorian English) displayed visually, so that I
understand how the language evolved to produce the word in context.

**US-017** — As a Scholar, I want anachronism flags on words or phrases that feel
incongruous for the period — with a brief editorial note explaining why — so that I
can appreciate the linguistic texture of translations, adaptations, or editorial choices.

---

## Epic 4 — Etymology & Historical Context

**US-018** — As a Scholar, I want inline contextual footnotes available for social
customs, institutions, and cultural references mentioned in a passage (e.g., "calling
card", "Season", "entail"), so that I understand the social world of the text.

**US-019** — As a Scholar, I want etymology cards that show root morphemes with
translations (e.g., "melancholy" ← Greek *melan* + *kholē*, "black bile"), so that I can
build a mental map of classical roots common in Victorian prose.

**US-020** — As a Scholar, I want the ability to see related works or authors referenced
or alluded to in a passage (e.g., a character quoting Milton), so that I can follow
literary threads deeper into the canon.

---

## Epic 5 — Collection (Quotes & Words)

**US-021** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want to save any highlighted passage as a favorite
quote — stored with book title, author, chapter, and page position — so that I can
revisit memorable lines and build my own anthology.

**US-022** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want to save individual words to a personal
"Vocabulary Notebook", with the context sentence they appeared in, so that my saved
words are meaningful and memorable rather than decontextualised.

**US-023** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want to tag and organize my saved quotes and
words (e.g., by book, theme, or custom tag), so that I can browse my collection
meaningfully.

**US-024** — As a Word Enthusiast, I want to export my saved quotes as a styled image
or share them formatted with author attribution, so that I can share them outside
the app while respecting Project Gutenberg's attribution requirements.

---

## Epic 6 — Flashcards & Quizzes

**US-025** — As a Quizzer, I want flashcard decks auto-generated from vocabulary I have
encountered while reading, so that review is always grounded in words from my
actual reading.

**US-026** — As a Quizzer, I want to create custom flashcard decks from words I have
manually saved to my Vocabulary Notebook, so that I can focus study on terms I care
most about.

**US-027** — As a Quizzer, I want multiple quiz formats — definition match, word from
definition, fill-in-the-blank from a real corpus passage, and odd-one-out — so that
my learning is varied and tests different dimensions of word knowledge.

**US-028** — As a Quizzer, I want spaced repetition applied to flashcard review, so
that words I find difficult appear more often and words I know well appear less.

**US-029** — As a Quizzer, I want quiz sessions to be visually styled as "examination
papers" or "correspondence letters" consistent with the Victorian aesthetic, so that
even drilling vocabulary feels immersive.

**US-030** — As a Quizzer, I want to see my quiz score history and accuracy rates per
word, so that I can track which vocabulary is consolidated and which needs work.

---

## Epic 7 — Visual Design & UX

**US-031** — As a Reader, I want the app's core aesthetic to evoke aged paper, copperplate
typography, ink illustrations, and Victorian decorative borders, so that the visual
design is of a piece with the literary content.

**US-032** — As a Reader, I want page transitions and panel reveals to use book-like
animations (page turn, paper unfold, wax-seal reveal), so that interactions feel
physically grounded in the era.

**US-033** — As a Reader, I want sound design to be optional and subtle — quill
scratching, paper rustling, an ambient library soundscape — so that immersion is
available but never forced.

---

## Epic 8 — Progress & Profile

**US-034** — As a Quizzer, I want to see a personal reading and learning dashboard
showing: books completed, words encountered, words consolidated, total reading
time, and active streaks, so that I have a clear sense of my progress.

**US-035** — As a Quizzer, I want achievements styled as Victorian medals, crests, and
illuminated certificates (e.g., "Order of the Inkwell", "Fellow of the Gothic Society"),
so that milestones feel rewarding and on-theme.

**US-036** — As a Reader, I want a reading streak tracker that encourages daily sessions,
with a visual metaphor of tending a candle or filling an inkwell, so that habit
formation is encouraged in a thematic way.
